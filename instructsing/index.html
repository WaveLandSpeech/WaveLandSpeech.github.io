INSTRUCTSING: GENERATIVE ADVERSARIAL NETWORK WITH MULTI-LEVEL SAMPLING RATE AUDIO FOR HIGH-FIDELITY SINGING VOICE GENERATION


Abstract

Training a high-performance neural vocoder is a time-consuming endeavor due to the substantial number of trainable parameters it necessitates. However, the presence of a large number of trainable parameters, in turn, results in a slower network convergence. To accelerate training while preserving the ability to generate high-quality singing voices, we introduce a novel neural vocoder called InstructSing. This vocoder leverages differentiable digital signal processing (DDSP) and a generative adversarial network (GAN) framework. It comprises a generator and two independent discriminators. The generator incorporates an Instruct Module, inspired by DDSP, which produces audio with an 8k sampling rate as an instructive signal. Additionally, it includes a UNet-based Bridge Module responsible for generating essential periodic and aperiodic information, along with a WaveNet-based ReverbNet Module for generating 48kHz high-fidelity singing voices. In terms of discriminators, we amalgamate a multi-period discriminator, as originally proposed in HiFiGAN, with a multi-scale multi-band Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) discriminator--an enhanced version of the multi-scale STFT discriminator featured in Encodec. Notably, InstructSing achieves convergence within 10,000 training steps on a 4-GPUs machine, outperforming other neural vocoders in both training speed and voice quality
